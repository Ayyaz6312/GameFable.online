Free Online Loan EMI Calculator — Calculate Any Loan in Seconds

Our free online loan EMI calculator helps you instantly determine your Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) for any type of loan — whether it's a home loan, car loan, personal loan, or education loan. Simply enter the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure to get accurate results with a detailed amortization schedule.

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each month. EMIs consist of two components: principal repayment and interest charged. In the initial months, a larger portion of the EMI goes toward interest, while in the later months, the principal component increases.

EMI Calculation Formula

The standard mathematical formula used by banks and financial institutions to calculate EMI is:

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)N] / [(1+R)N − 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount (the total amount borrowed)
  • R = Monthly interest rate = (Annual rate / 12) / 100
  • N = Total number of monthly installments (loan tenure in months)

Types of Loans You Can Calculate

Our calculator supports four major loan types commonly used worldwide:

  • Home Loan / Mortgage: Typically 15–30 years, interest rates 5–10%. Used for purchasing residential property. Home loans usually have the lowest interest rates due to the collateral (the property itself).
  • Car Loan / Auto Loan: Typically 3–7 years, interest rates 5–15%. Used for purchasing new or used vehicles. The car serves as collateral.
  • Personal Loan: Typically 1–5 years, interest rates 10–25%. Unsecured loan for personal expenses like weddings, travel, medical emergencies, or debt consolidation.
  • Education Loan: Typically 5–15 years, interest rates 6–15%. Designed for students to cover tuition, living expenses, and study materials. Many offer a moratorium period.

What is an Amortization Schedule?

An amortization schedule is a detailed table showing every monthly payment throughout the loan tenure. For each month, it breaks down how much of your EMI goes toward principal repayment and how much goes toward interest payment. It also shows the remaining loan balance after each payment. This helps you understand how your loan is being repaid over time and plan for prepayments if desired.

Tips to Reduce Your Loan EMI

  • Increase your down payment — Reduces the principal amount, lowering both EMI and total interest
  • Choose a longer tenure — Spreads the repayment over more months, reducing monthly EMI (but increases total interest)
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate — Even a 0.25% reduction can save significant money over long tenures
  • Make prepayments — Extra payments toward principal reduce the outstanding balance and future interest
  • Improve your credit score — A higher credit score qualifies you for better interest rates from lenders
  • Compare lenders — Different banks and NBFCs offer different rates; always shop around before finalizing
